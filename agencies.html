Government

Departments & Agencies

The programs and services the county operates for residents every day.

Mercer County 911 Emergencies: 911 The Mercer Communications Center is the Public Safety Answering & Dispatch Point for police, fire, EMS and emergency management countywide — processing 911 and non-emergency calls promptly and professionally to help save lives and protect property. Emergency Management (OES) Disaster preparedness, response coordination and public-safety planning for Mercer County. Animal Shelter (304) 425-2838 · 961 Shelter Road, Princeton An open-admission county shelter (Director Stacy Harman) providing humane care for stray and relinquished animals. Accepts animals from county residents; all pets are spayed/neutered before adoption; rescue-friendly with 501(c)(3) partners. Health Department Clinic: Blue Prince Family Health Empowers residents to live healthy lives in healthy communities. Full clinic services via Blue Prince Family Health: primary and preventative care, wellness and cancer screenings, immunizations and vaccines. Mercer County Airport (304) 327-8440 · 300 Markell Dr, Ste 201, Bluefield General-aviation airport (Manager Jim Pilkins) serving the greater Bluefield/Princeton region under the oversight of the County Commission. Recycling Program 749 Frontage Road, Princeton (Landfill) Drop-off recycling at the Sanitary Landfill. Hours: Mon–Fri 7 AM–5 PM, Sat 8 AM–1 PM. Accepts #1 & #2 plastics, ferrous metals & cans, all aluminum, and bagged paper / broken-down cardboard (materials must be separated). Day Report Center Community-based alternative sentencing and supervision programs. Corrections / Regional Jail Coordination with the Southern Regional Jail and inmate services. Adult Probation Supervision of adults on probation within the judicial circuit. Juvenile Probation Supervision and services for juveniles under court jurisdiction. Floodplain Management Floodplain permitting and compliance with FEMA/NFIP development standards. Litter Control / Keep Mercer Clean The county's nationally recognized, award-winning litter-control and community-cleanup initiative. Mercer County Park & Parks County park facilities, ballfields, pool and recreation across the county. Convention & Visitors Bureau visitmercercounty.com Promotes tourism and travel to Mercer County. Development Authority Economic development, business recruitment and workforce initiatives. County Fair The annual Mercer County Fair and associated community events. Education / Schools Links to Mercer County Schools and WVU Extension services. Hatfield-McCoy Trails Access to the region's renowned ATV/OHV trail system.
Cities, towns & partners

Municipalities & partner agencies

Incorporated communities and partner organizations that serve Mercer County residents.

Town of Athens Athens is a community in Mercer County, West Virginia, home to Concord University. Municipal documents and information are posted on the official county page. City of Bluefield Known as "Nature's Air-Conditioned City," Bluefield, West Virginia blends small-town charm with a rich history rooted in the railroad and coal industries, offering cool summers, outdoor adventures, and a vibrant community with Bluefield State College. Town of Bramwell A picturesque historical town nestled along the Bluestone National Scenic River in Mercer County, West Virginia, established in 1888 and once noted for having the greatest number of millionaires per capita in the United States. Mercer County Commission The Mercer County Commission is the elected governing body of the county, responsible for fiscal affairs, county levies, property administration, probate, and appointing members to county boards and authorities. The commission holds at least four regular public sessions per year at the courthouse. Glenwood Recreational Park Glenwood Recreational Park is a lakeside park on Rt. 20 between Princeton and Bluewell offering fishing, boating, hiking, mountain biking, miniature golf, disc golf, picnic shelters, a wedding gazebo, and access to the adjacent Mercer County 4-H Camp. Town of Oakvale The Town of Oakvale document page hosts municipal documents and information; check the official county site for current details. City of Princeton The City of Princeton, known as the "Heart of Mercer County," is the region's economic and cultural hub, featuring historic downtown architecture, locally owned shops, restaurants, and access to Appalachian Mountain recreational opportunities. Sheriff's Office The Mercer County Sheriff's Office document page hosts department documents and forms; see the Sheriff's detail page and the official county site for current information. WVU Extension — Mercer County The West Virginia University Extension office in Mercer County provides research-based programs in agriculture, 4-H youth development, food and nutrition, health and wellness, financial planning, and home gardening to all county residents.
