About the shelter

Humane care, every single day

The Mercer County Animal Shelter is an open-admission county facility led by Director Stacy Harman. We provide shelter, food and veterinary care to lost, stray and relinquished animals, and we work every day to reunite pets with their families or place them in loving new homes.

  • Open-admission — we accept animals from Mercer County residents
  • Every adopted pet is spayed or neutered before going home
  • Rescue-friendly — we partner with 501(c)(3) rescue organizations
  • Adopters receive vaccination and microchipping guidance

Adopt

How to adopt a pet

Adopting a shelter pet saves a life — and opens a kennel for another animal in need. Here's how the process works.

1 · Meet the animals

Call or visit the shelter to see the dogs and cats currently available. Staff can help match a pet to your home and lifestyle.

2 · Apply

Complete a short adoption application. Staff review it to ensure a safe, lasting placement for the animal.

3 · Spay / neuter

Every pet is spayed or neutered before adoption — a cornerstone of responsible, humane pet ownership.

4 · Take them home

Finalize the adoption, get vaccination and microchip guidance, and welcome your new family member home.

Get involved

More ways to help

You don't have to adopt to make a difference. Fostering, volunteering and donations keep our animals healthy and cared for.

Foster a pet

Open your home temporarily to a pet who needs extra time, socialization or medical recovery. Fostering frees up space and saves lives.

Volunteer

Walk dogs, socialize cats, help at events or lend a professional skill. Call the shelter to learn about current volunteer needs.

Donate

Food, blankets, cleaning supplies and monetary gifts all help. Call ahead to confirm the shelter's current wish list before dropping off items.

Rescue partners

We work with 501(c)(3) rescue organizations to place animals. Rescues interested in partnering are encouraged to reach out.

Lost & found

Lost or found a pet?

If your pet is missing, contact the shelter as soon as possible and check in regularly — reuniting families is one of the most important things we do. If you've found a stray, call us so we can help.

  • Call (304) 425-2838 to report a lost or found animal
  • Have a description, photo and the location last seen ready
  • Microchipped pets are far easier to reunite — ask your vet about chipping
Surrendering an animal

Need to surrender a pet?

As an open-admission shelter, we accept animals from Mercer County residents. If you can no longer care for a pet, please call ahead so staff can guide you through intake and, where possible, discuss alternatives that might keep your pet with you.
