Mercer County Noise Ordinance

This ordinance prohibits unnecessary, excessive, and annoying noise disturbances in Mercer County, specifying restrictions on amplified sound, yelling, and animal noise, and setting penalties escalating from $5–$50 for a first offense to $100–$500 or up to 90 days in jail for a third or subsequent offense.

Enacted September 13, 2005, the ordinance recognizes that noise at certain levels is detrimental to the health, welfare, safety, and quality of life of county inhabitants and declares it county policy to reduce noise and prohibit unnecessary, excessive, and annoying noise from all sources subject to its police power. It is unlawful for any person to knowingly keep or harbor any animal that habitually barks, howls, cries, or yelps to the great discomfort of the neighborhood, with such animals declared a public nuisance. Complaints trigger a warning from the Sheriff's Department; if ineffective, a verified complaint signed by at least two citizens (not from the same household) or a deputy sheriff may be filed with a magistrate, and the owner cited.

A 'noise disturbance' is defined as any sound that by its character, intensity, and duration endangers or injures health or safety, or annoys or disturbs persons of normal sensitivities. Specific prohibitions include: operating amplified audio equipment, televisions, musical instruments, or similar devices between 12:00 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. in a manner audible across a residential property boundary, or operating such devices in or on a motor vehicle on a public right-of-way at a volume audible more than 50 feet away; and yelling, shouting, whistling, or singing between 12:00 a.m. and 7:00 a.m. on a public right-of-way, or at any time across a residential property boundary or in a manner disturbing occupants of any office, hotel, or motel.

Exemptions include sounds necessary for protection of property or life; police, fire, and emergency vehicle sirens and horns; parades, fireworks, school-related activities and other special events; activities on county and school athletic facilities authorized by the property owner; fire and burglar alarms (with notice and reasonable opportunity to shut off); religious services including music, bells, chimes, and organs; locomotives and aircraft; the striking of clocks; and any noise from activities permitted by law or county or state license. As an additional remedy, noise disturbances may be abated by restraining order or injunction. Penalties: first offense $5–$50; second offense $25–$100; third or subsequent offense $100–$500 and/or up to 90 days in county jail.

Adopted: September 13, 2005
