Elected Offices & Courts

Constitutional offices and courts that serve Mercer County residents.

Assessor (304) 487-8397 Courthouse · 1501 W Main St, Princeton Fairly values all real estate and personal property in the county — homes, land, commercial buildings and vehicles — the basis for property taxes. Provides homestead, veterans and senior exemptions plus online property records and tax maps.
County Clerk (304) 487-8338 Suite 121, Courthouse · Princeton Fiscal officer and custodian of official records: elections and voter registration, probate, vital records (birth, death, marriage), hunting & fishing licenses, notary and civil-service services, payroll and county budgeting. Photo ID is required to vote in WV.
Circuit Clerk Courthouse · Princeton A constitutional office (WV Const. Art. 8, §9), elected to six-year terms. Registrar, recorder and custodian of all Circuit and Family Court pleadings, manages the county jury system, and assists self-represented litigants.
Sheriff & Tax Office Courthouse · Princeton Law enforcement, service of civil process, operation of the county jail, and collection of real-estate and personal-property taxes. Blends time-honored values with modern public-safety strategies to safeguard the community.
Prosecuting Attorney Courthouse · Princeton Prosecutes felony and misdemeanor criminal cases on behalf of the State of West Virginia and advises county government, working closely with law enforcement and the courts.
Circuit Court Judges Courthouse · Princeton The Circuit Court hears felony criminal cases and major civil matters for the county's judicial circuit.
Family Court Judges Courthouse · Princeton Handles divorce, custody, child and spousal support, and other domestic-relations matters.
Magistrate Court Magistrate Court · Princeton Handles misdemeanors, small claims, arrest and search warrants, and emergency protective orders.
