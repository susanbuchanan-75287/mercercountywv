Ordinances & County Code

Regulatory orders and ordinances adopted by the Mercer County Commission. Where these summaries differ from the official record, the adopted ordinance and applicable law control.

Abandoned and Dilapidated Building Ordinance

This ordinance addresses abandoned and severely dilapidated buildings declared public nuisances and hazards to citizens of Mercer County, establishing an enforcement agency and procedures for abatement.

Adopted March 23, 2021Read summary →

Exotic Entertainment Restriction Ordinance

This ordinance restricts the location of businesses offering exotic entertainment in Mercer County to at least 2,000 feet from churches, schools, parks, residential properties, family-oriented restaurants, licensed alcohol premises, and lodging establishments.

Adopted November 12, 2002Read summary →

Fireworks Discharge Restriction Ordinance

This ordinance restricts the days, hours, and proximity within which consumer fireworks may be discharged in unincorporated Mercer County, and requires temporary retail vendors to register with the County Commission and pay a $100 fee.

Adopted May 14, 2019; effective June 1, 2019Read summary →

Mental Hygiene – Involuntary Hospitalization (WV Code § 27-5-1 et seq.)

The page reproduces West Virginia State Code Chapter 27, Article 5, governing involuntary hospitalization proceedings, including the appointment and duties of mental hygiene commissioners, prosecuting attorneys, and sheriffs in Mercer County mental hygiene matters.

Adopted Effective June 5, 2020 (§ 27-5-1, Acts 2020, c. 273); current with legislation of the 2020 Regular SessionRead summary →

Mercer County Canine Control Ordinance

This ordinance prohibits dog owners in unincorporated Mercer County from allowing their dogs to run at large, authorizes Animal Control Officers and law enforcement to seize and impound dogs found at large, and establishes fines of $25–$100 per violation.

Adopted Amended May 6, 2025; entered June 24, 2025Read summary →

Mercer County Floodplain Ordinance

This ordinance establishes a floodplain area in Mercer County and requires permits for all development, construction, substantial improvement, or relocation of buildings or structures within that area, setting minimum construction standards and penalties for non-compliance.

Read summary →

Mercer County Litter Control Ordinance

This ordinance prohibits littering on public and private property in Mercer County, establishes duties for property owners and businesses to maintain litter-free premises, and sets penalties ranging from $50 to $500 with the option of community service.

Adopted December 9, 2008Read summary →

Mercer County Noise Ordinance

This ordinance prohibits unnecessary, excessive, and annoying noise disturbances in Mercer County, specifying restrictions on amplified sound, yelling, and animal noise, and setting penalties escalating from $5–$50 for a first offense to $100–$500 or up to 90 days in jail for a third or subsequent offense.

Adopted September 13, 2005Read summary →

Mercer County Spay Neuter Ordinance

This ordinance requires residents to spay or neuter all dogs and cats over six months of age kept in Mercer County, with exceptions for licensed breeders and specific medical or situational exemptions, and establishes an Unaltered Animal License (UAL) system enforced by Animal Control.

Adopted June 10, 2025Read summary →
