Mercer County Canine Control Ordinance

This ordinance prohibits dog owners in unincorporated Mercer County from allowing their dogs to run at large, authorizes Animal Control Officers and law enforcement to seize and impound dogs found at large, and establishes fines of $25–$100 per violation.

Enacted under West Virginia Code § 19-20-6 and amended May 6, 2025 (entered June 24, 2025), the ordinance applies to all dogs in the unincorporated areas of Mercer County. Owners must confine dogs on their premises in a humane manner consistent with WV Code § 61-8-19, which requires proper sustenance (food, water, shelter from the elements) and prohibits cruel chaining or tethering. No dog may be taken onto streets, sidewalks, or public places unless on a leash or otherwise under physical control. Exceptions are made for 'farm dogs' protecting livestock and 'hunting dogs' engaged in lawful hunting while equipped with an active tracking device being followed by the owner.

A dog is deemed 'at large' whenever it is not under the immediate physical control of a responsible person capable of restraining it and is not on the owner's property or property where it has express permission to be. Animal Control Officers, Dog Wardens, and law enforcement officers are required to seize any dog found at large—whether licensed or unlicensed—and impound it consistent with WV Code §§ 19-20-6 and 19-20-8. Cruel tethering is specifically defined and prohibited, including use of restraints exceeding one-eighth the animal's body weight, restraints that cause choking, or restraints under approximately 15 feet in length that prevent the animal from urinating or defecating in a separate area.

An owner may redeem an impounded dog within five days of notice by paying all assessed costs and any fine, after which the owner must obtain a license from the courthouse and provide a current rabies vaccination per WV Code § 19-20A-2. Violation is a misdemeanor; fines range from $25 to $100 (in addition to court costs). Each incident of a dog being at large in any 12-hour period constitutes a single violation.

Adopted: Amended May 6, 2025; entered June 24, 2025
